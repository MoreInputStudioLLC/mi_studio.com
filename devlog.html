Devlog

How the dungeon got built

Letters & Liches - Array clawed its way out of one random evening where Heather collaborated with Claude to cure her boredom. Here's the descent, milestone by milestone.

Word DungeonMarch 2026
Early March 2026

The Lost History

The first build of Word Dungeon was recovered but the date was lost. Nobody knows when the concept landed. Early March? Late February? Mysterious...

March 7, 2026

It Lived

File recovered but needed some rehab to be enjoyed. Letters could be all consonants, maps could be un-traversable, once hit there was no recovering — but opportunities were being dreamed up.

March 8, 2026

One Monster Session

A Merchant selling healing potions, the ability to get New Letters, solid maps with reachable end points, a Dungeon Sovereign boss waiting at depth 3. The fire was lit, and Claude made the pace and scope possible.

March 11, 2026

Versioning

While earlier versions were lost, version 11 was where the project started coming into focus. Could this actually be something? Word Dungeon already existed so that's priority one.

Letters & LichesThe name that stuck
March 12, 2026

Letters & Liches

Lots of proposals, lots of vetoes, finally a name landed for us! Alliterative, a little gothic, exactly right. Everything since has been building the dungeon out from there.

Early days

Lost to the Dark

Wander into a dead end with no way out and the dungeon doesn't just end your run — it swallows you whole. A favorite bit of early cruelty.

Taking shape

Bosses, potions & a merchant

A boss guarding every depth. Health potions that auto-fire the moment you're hit. And a merchant who'll sell you an extra tile — if you can cover it.

Difficulty

Modes for every mood

Casual for a relaxed spell, Standard for the full climb, and Hard — where a ticking word timer means hesitation costs blood.

Out of OrderMeanwhile
March 29, 2026

Gone camping

Sometimes the most important dev tool is stepping away from the keyboard. The log went quiet for a bit. It was worth it.

Housekeeping

The great dictionary purge

Discovery: 75 proper names hiding in the word list. Sorry, "Arthur" — you're not a common noun. Out you go.

April 13, 2026

The build that wasn't

Version 46 was a disaster — broken systems top to bottom. The decision was made to throw it out and rebuild from 45. Keeping this one in the log for honesty: not every version ships.

A voice for the dark

Sound & music

The slash on every hit: satisfying, first try, still there. The ambient score is a generated loop holding the atmosphere in place while a composed track from Bronson Cole takes shape. The dungeon is patient.

April 17, 2026

L&L — Array: Alpha Test

One special visitor, and one of the first to try L&L — Array: my musical younger brother, Bronson Cole. The visit was centered on relocating furniture rather than traveling a dungeon, however.

May 1, 2026

3 headsets, 2 pets, 1 mom, 0 time

We packed the dog, the cat, work computers, and three VR headsets into the car and drove 700 miles to an airbnb in our hometown for a working holiday. Real minigolf together. VR minigolf with family. My mom rode home with us and stayed through mid-May. Life ran full; the game ran slower.

Enter HexehA guide in the gloom
May 2026

Hexeh arrives

An arduous build over many weeks resulted in a beloved witch to walk you through your first descent. She may gift you a valuable incantation and will reward your cheekiness if you spell her name. This character was inspired by a wonderful straight-talking hard-ass named Jean who took in a gaggle of us miscreant teenagers in the late 90s. RIP.

A Studio NowAll the polish
Art pass

The monsters get faces

Emoji placeholders gave way to real creature portraits — liches, wyrms, and worse. Creature and other portraits — art direction via ChatGPT and finishing in Corel PaintShop Pro by Joshua Gates.

June 30, 2026

The studio steps forward

A logo had been dreamed up and crudely made by me in Paint on April 6th. Old friend Inkscape got it the rest of the way to the official studio version, which made its way into the game along with proper credits in early July.

The tiny jackhammer

A soundtrack, eventually

The looping score first shipped as a "fast repeating thud with no melody." One of the audio tools was quietly producing near-silence. Fixed with a better encoder and a lot of squinting at waveforms.

July 16, 2026

In the weeds

Full creature portraits queued up for inclusion, a seamless looping score functioning while the real stuff is being created, and a demo taking shape. It's almost time to play.
